## Known residual risks

1. **Indefinite retention vs the GDPR storage-limitation principle (Art. 5(1)(e)).** "Retain while relevant, delete on request" is defensible only if "relevance" is genuinely assessed; old mentee requests and reviews with no ongoing purpose accumulate indefinitely by default. This is the policy's weakest GDPR point (acknowledged in D13). Mitigation to consider: a periodic (e.g. annual) manual relevance sweep, or a future scheduled anonymization job.
2. **No professional legal review has occurred.** These documents were drafted and reviewed in-house by an AI. Strongly recommend a professional review (Dutch/EU privacy + consumer law) before significant scale, before any monetization, or before expanding stored data categories.
3. **Individual as controller.** The owner is personally the data controller: personal liability for GDPR violations, personal exposure to DPA complaints and data-subject claims, and no corporate liability shield. Moving to a legal entity (e.g. a Dutch B.V. or eenmanszaak registration) should be revisited as the service grows.
4. **DPAs and transfer paperwork not confirmed.** The policy asserts SCCs/DPF for US-linked providers (Cloudflare, Google, Grafana Labs, AWS entities); the actual data-processing agreements with each provider still need to be accepted/verified (ROPA open item). Grafana Cloud EU-region confirmation is still open.
5. **GTM container contents are outside code control.** The policy says GTM loads only with consent (true in code), but what GTM loads is configured in the GTM console; the Mixpanel tag removal there is a manual owner task (D18). A stray tag in the container would make the cookies section inaccurate.
6. **Reviews rest on legitimate interest** (Art. 6(1)(f)) without a documented balancing test (LIA). Low risk at current scale, but worth writing down if challenged.
